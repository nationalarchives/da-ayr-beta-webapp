Cookie policy

Last updated on 10 January 2025.

Cookies

Cookies are small files saved on your computer, phone or tablet that allow us to store information about how you use our website, such as the pages you visit or any preferences you set. They help us to provide you with a better experience. Read more about cookies used on The National Archives website.

Within the AYR – Access Your Records service, we only use cookies that are essential in order to make the service work. They cannot be used to identify you personally.

Why do we use cookies?

  • Enabling a service to recognise your device so you don’t have to give the same information several times during one task
  • Recognising that you may already have provided a username and password so you don’t need to do it for every web page requested

We classify the cookies we use within the AYR – Access Your Records service in the following categories:

  • Analytics cookies (optional, although none are currently used within the AYR – Access Your Records service)
  • Functional (optional, although none are currently used within the AYR – Access Your Records service)
  • Essential cookies (non-optional)

We do not use any cookies for marketing or targeting purposes.

Third party cookies

These are cookies set by a website other than nationalarchives.gov.uk. They are set when you click on a third party link such as SmartSurvey. You cannot set your preferences for these on our site, but you can block them directly from your browser.
