    How to use the Access Your Records Service

    Last updated on 15 April 2024.

    Getting started

    What is AYR

    Access Your Records (AYR) is a secure digital access service from The National Archives (TNA) that enables public record bodies to search, browse and download public records. Only digital records that have been transferred to TNA for permanent preservation via the Transfer Digital Records (TDR) service will be made available through AYR. Unlike the Discovery catalogue, you can access records that are both open and closed using this service.

    Accessing my AYR account

    What browsers are supported?

    We do not support Internet Explorer. We advise you to use the latest browser version of Edge, Chrome or Firefox to ensure you can use this service and all of its features.

    Which email should I use to sign in?

    Use the email used to create your account. If your email address changes, send full details of the change to tdr@nationalarchives.gov.uk

    My password does not work

    If you forget your password or need to reset it, select the ‘Forgot Your Password?' link on the sign-in page to request a reset and follow the on-screen instructions. For any other password issue, email tdr@nationalarchives.gov.uk.

    What is Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)?

    To keep the service and your account secure, you are required to sign in each time using Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA). This uses two separate pieces of information to authenticate and grant access to the service. In AYR you must sign in each time using your password and a unique generated ‘one-time passcode’ (OTP) that confirms who you are. This can be set up with a free-to-download authenticator app on your smartphone device, such as Google Authenticator, Microsoft Authenticator, or FreeOTP. Other authenticators are also available.

    Free-to-download apps are available from the App Store for iPhone, or Google Play for Android.

    How do I set up MFA and my one-time passcode (OTP)?

    When you register as a new AYR user, follow the registration steps in the User Help Guide to set up an AYR account in the authenticator app on a smartphone that you have access to. You only need to configure this once for it to generate a code for each time you sign in.

    Where do I get my OTP code?

    In the authenticator app, select the AYR account that you set up and you'll see an OTP generated every 30 to 60 seconds. Ensure you have enough time to use the code to complete the sign-in process before the code refreshes.

    When setting up the authenticator app, my configuration failed - what should I do?

    If the authentication app fails to configure when adding a new account for AYR on your device, delete the failed AYR account within the app and try again. For guidance on how to do this on each device, follow the instructions below.

    For Android:

    1. Open the authenticator app on the device and tap 'Generate code'.
    2. On the 'Generate code' screen, press and hold the account you want to remove.
    3. When prompted, tap remove.

    For iOS:

    1. Open the authenticator app on the device and tap 'Generate code'.
    2. On the 'Generate code' screen, press the account you wish to remove and swipe it to the left.
    3. If you are using the Microsoft Authenticator, look for the settings cog in the top corner and tap it to see the option that says ‘Remove account’.

    If you need further support on the app, your local IT support desk may be able to assist.

    I am not able to use my phone camera to scan the QR code

    Some device users may need to enable camera access in Settings.

    Alternatively, a 9-digit key code can be manually entered into the app. Once you have the code, you can enter it into the app. To do this:

    1. Select the 'I want to enter a key instead' link underneath the QR code displayed on the page to generate the code.
    2. Within your authenticator app select the 'Enter a setup key' option.
    3. Enter the key code as shown into the app to create your unique OTP account for TDR sign-in.
    4. You should now be able to see a 6-digit code that will refresh at regular intervals.

    My account registration link has expired

    If your ‘Update Your Account’ link has expired before use, email tdr@nationalarchives.gov.uk from your work email account and ask for a new link. Allow up to two working days for your request to be processed.

    Can I share my sign-in information?

    You must not share your personal sign-in information with anyone else. Always keep your details safe and secure.

    Finding records using search and browse

    How do I search for records?

    AYR allows you to search for any records that you have access to. You can view records based on transferring body or department, series, the TDR consignment reference, the date the record was transferred to TNA or the file name. AYR holds both open and closed digital records.

    To search for a record, enter a file name, transferring body, series or consignment reference into the search bar and select ‘Search’.

    How do I browse through records?

    AYR will recognise the department you belong to when you sign in to the service. The browse section will display all the records available to you. You can select through transferring body, series or TDR consignment references to find records. Alternatively, you can filter the results by transferring body, series or consignment reference using the filter panel on the right side of the screen. You can also limit records by the date the record was transferred to TNA via TDR or the last date the record was modified.

    I cannot find my records

    AYR is a read-only system that allows users to search for digital records that have been transferred via TDR. If you cannot find a record using the search or browse functions, contact TDR or a record transfer advisor to see if there was a problem with your digital transfer. You can also use the Discovery catalogue to search for open digital records and records transferred via a hard drive which are not available on the AYR service.

    How do I view my records?

    When you select a record to view, the service will display any available metadata that was included during the record transfer via TDR. This includes open and closed file name, descriptions, and closure status. AYR will not make changes or updates to transferred metadata, even if it is incorrect.

    Can I download records?

    AYR allows you to download the records you have permission to access. Some of these records may be over 20 years old and the software to open them may no longer exist. There is currently no preview option in AYR to view records before downloading.
