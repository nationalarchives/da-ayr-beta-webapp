    Privacy Notice for Access Your Records

    Last updated on 15 April 2024.

    What data we collect from you

    The personal data we collect from you will include:

    • your name
    • your work email address

    The legal basis under UK GDPR for processing this data is:

    • legal obligation
    • legitimate interests

    The National Archives (TNA) requires you to sign in to the system so that we can provide the correct level of access and monitor which documents have been accessed.

    Why we need your data

    We need your data to:

    • make sure we have accurate information about who accesses digital records and which records are accessed
    • maintain the safety and security of all sensitive records

    What we do with your data

    AYR is a record access system. The data processed is used by:

    • Keycloak (authentication software)
    • Access Your Records Service

    We will not:

    • sell or rent your data to third parties
    • share your data with third parties for marketing purposes

    We will share your data if we’re required to do so by law – for example, by court order, or to prevent fraud or other crime.

    How long we keep your data for

    We will only keep your personal data for as long as:

    • the law requires us to
    • we need it for the purposes listed above

    We will regularly review our user list and remove any accounts that have been inactive for one year or when we have been notified that you have left your department.

    Where your data is processed and stored

    All personal data is processed, stored and managed entirely in the United Kingdom, and will not be transferred outside of it. This means that it is covered by UK data protection regulations.

    How we protect your data and keep it secure

    We are committed to doing all that we can to keep your data secure. We set up systems and processes to prevent unauthorised access or disclosure of the data we collect about you – for example, we protect your data using varying levels of encryption.

    Your rights

    You have the right to request:

    • information about how your personal data is processed
    • a copy of that personal data – this copy will be provided in a structured, commonly used and machine-readable format
    • that anything inaccurate in your personal data is corrected immediately

    You can also:

    • raise an objection about how your personal data is processed
    • request that your personal data is erased if there is no longer a justification for it
    • ask that we only process your data in certain circumstances to limit how we use your data

    Changes to this notice

    We may change this privacy notice and any changes will apply to you and your data immediately. If these changes affect how your personal data is processed, TNA will take reasonable steps to make sure you know. When we make changes to this notice, the ‘last updated’ date at the top of this page will reflect when the change is made and effective from.

    Questions and complaints

    Contact the Data Protection Officer, Linda Stewart, if you:

    • have any questions about anything in this document
    • think that your personal data has been misused or mishandled
    • want to make a subject access request (SAR)

    By email:

    You can contact us by email at tdr@nationalarchives.gov.uk

    By live chat:

    You can also use our live online chat service which is open Tuesday to Saturday, between 09:00 and 17:00. Refer to The National Archives contact page for details.
