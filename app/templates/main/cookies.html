Cookies

Cookies are small files saved on your computer, phone or tablet that allow us to store information about how you use our website, such as the pages you visit or any preferences you set. They help us to provide you with a better experience. Read more about cookies used on The National Archives website.

Within the {{ config['SERVICE_NAME'] }} service, we only use cookies that are essential in order to make the service work. They cannot be used to identify you personally.

Why do we use cookies?

We use cookies to improve the services that we provide and to ensure they work for you by:

  • Enabling a service to recognise your device so you don’t have to give the same information several times during one task
  • Recognising that you may already have provided a username and password so you don’t need to do it for every web page requested

We classify the cookies we use within the {{ config['SERVICE_NAME'] }} service in the following categories:

  • Analytics cookies (optional, although none are currently used within the {{ config['SERVICE_NAME'] }} service)
  • Functional (optional, although none are currently used within the {{ config['SERVICE_NAME'] }} service)
  • Essential cookies (non-optional)

We do not use any cookies for marketing or targeting purposes.

Essential cookies

These are necessary to make our site work. You can disable them by changing your browser preferences. However, you should be aware that doing so will mean some parts of the website will stop working for you.

Cookie name What it does/typical content Duration
session A cookie that holds information for authorisation purpose Expires when you exit the browser

Third party cookies

These are cookies set by a website other than nationalarchives.gov.uk. They are set when you click on a third party link such as SmartSurvey. You cannot set your preferences for these on our site, but you can block them directly from your browser.
